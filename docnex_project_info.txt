-- ABSTRACT --------------------------------------------------------

DocNex is an AI-powered healthcare document management and doctor recommendation platform designed to bridge the gap between patients and specialist doctors across AIIMS hospitals in India. The platform addresses the critical problem faced by millions of Indian patients who receive complex medical reports but lack the expertise to interpret them or identify the right specialist for follow-up treatment.

The system leverages modern web technologies including React 19 with TypeScript for the frontend, Tailwind CSS 4 for responsive dark-themed styling, Firebase Authentication for secure Google Sign-In, and the Groq Cloud AI API with the Meta LLaMA 3.3 70B Versatile large language model for intelligent medical report analysis. PDF text extraction is handled client-side using the Mozilla pdfjs-dist library, ensuring patient data privacy by avoiding unnecessary server-side file storage.

Key features implemented in DocNex include AI-powered PDF medical report analysis that extracts patient demographics, symptoms, diagnosed conditions, abnormal findings, critical values, medications, and key findings from uploaded reports. The system provides a severity and urgency classification (mild/moderate/severe and routine/urgent/emergency) and automatically recommends the most appropriate specialist type and department. A multi-field scoring algorithm then matches the AI-extracted medical data against a curated dataset of 20 specialist doctors across 7 AIIMS campuses in India (New Delhi, Mumbai, Bhopal, Jodhpur, Rishikesh, Patna, and Bhubaneswar), returning the top 3 best-matching doctors with their full contact details, OPD schedules, and exact hospital locations.

The platform serves two user roles: patients who can upload records, analyze reports, find doctors, and book appointments, and doctors who can manage their schedules, view appointments, and track patient consultations. All pages feature a consistent premium dark-themed UI with smooth animations, glassmorphism effects, and responsive design that works across desktop and mobile devices.

DocNex contributes directly to United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 3 (Good Health and Well-Being) by making specialist healthcare more accessible to patients across India, regardless of their medical literacy level. The platform demonstrates that AI-powered NLP can effectively extract structured medical information from unstructured PDF reports and translate it into actionable doctor recommendations, potentially reducing the time patients spend searching for the right specialist from days to seconds.


-- CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION ----------------------------------------

1.1 BACKGROUND

The healthcare industry in India faces a significant challenge in managing and interpreting medical documents. With over 1.4 billion people and a doctor-to-patient ratio of approximately 1:1000, the Indian healthcare system is under immense pressure. Patients frequently receive medical reports from diagnostic labs and hospitals but lack the medical knowledge to understand complex pathological findings, radiology results, or blood work interpretations.

The All India Institutes of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) network, comprising over 20 campuses across India, represents the premier tier of public healthcare institutions. However, patients often struggle to identify which AIIMS department or specialist is most appropriate for their specific condition. This leads to unnecessary delays in treatment, multiple incorrect referrals, and wasted time in wrong OPD queues.

The rapid advancement of Artificial Intelligence, particularly Large Language Models (LLMs), has opened new possibilities for healthcare document analysis. Models like Meta's LLaMA 3.3 70B can now understand medical terminology, extract structured data from unstructured text, and provide clinically relevant recommendations. Cloud-based AI inference services like Groq provide ultra-fast processing of these models, making real-time medical report analysis feasible in web applications.

Modern web technologies including React, TypeScript, and Tailwind CSS enable the development of responsive, accessible, and visually appealing healthcare interfaces. Firebase Authentication provides enterprise-grade security for patient data, while client-side PDF processing libraries like pdfjs-dist ensure that sensitive medical documents never leave the patient's browser unnecessarily.

DocNex was conceived at this intersection of healthcare needs and technological capability, aiming to create an intelligent bridge between complex medical reports and appropriate specialist doctors at AIIMS hospitals across India.

1.2 PROBLEM STATEMENT

The DocNex project addresses the following critical problems in Indian healthcare:

Problem 1 - Medical Report Comprehension Gap: Patients receive complex medical reports containing pathological values, radiological findings, and clinical observations written in medical terminology. Most patients cannot interpret these reports independently, leading to anxiety, misunderstanding, and delayed treatment.

Problem 2 - Specialist Identification Difficulty: Even when patients understand their condition, they struggle to identify the right type of specialist (cardiologist, pulmonologist, neurologist, etc.) and the specific AIIMS campus that has the relevant department. This results in wasted appointments and delayed diagnosis.

Problem 3 - Lack of Digitized Record Management: Many patients in India still manage their medical records as physical paper documents. There is no widely available free platform that allows patients to upload, store, and organize their medical records digitally with AI-powered insights.

Problem 4 - Absence of Intelligent Doctor Recommendation: Existing healthcare platforms in India primarily offer doctor listings based on location and specialization but do not analyze the patient's actual medical report to recommend the most appropriate specialist. There is no system that reads a patient's PDF report, understands the medical findings, and maps them to specific AIIMS doctors.

Problem 5 - Fragmented Healthcare Experience: Patients currently use separate tools for storing records, understanding diagnoses, finding doctors, and booking appointments. There is no unified platform that connects these workflows into a seamless experience.

1.3 OBJECTIVES

The primary objectives of the DocNex project are:

Objective 1: To develop an AI-powered PDF medical report analysis system using the Groq Cloud API with the LLaMA 3.3 70B Versatile model that can extract patient demographics, symptoms, diagnosed conditions, abnormal findings, critical values, medications, key findings, severity level, urgency classification, and recommended specialist type from uploaded medical reports.

Objective 2: To implement an intelligent AIIMS doctor recommendation engine using a multi-field keyword scoring algorithm that matches AI-extracted medical data against a curated dataset of 20 specialist doctors across 7 AIIMS campuses in India (New Delhi, Mumbai, Bhopal, Jodhpur, Rishikesh, Patna, and Bhubaneswar).

Objective 3: To build a secure patient record management system with Google Authentication via Firebase that allows patients to upload, view, and manage their medical records in a digital vault with HIPAA-compliant security principles.

Objective 4: To create a comprehensive healthcare web application with role-based access control supporting both Patient and Doctor user roles, each with dedicated dashboards, navigation, and feature sets.

Objective 5: To design a premium, accessible, dark-themed user interface using React 19, TypeScript, and Tailwind CSS 4 that provides a seamless experience across desktop and mobile devices with smooth animations and modern design patterns.

Objective 6: To demonstrate the practical application of Large Language Models in healthcare by processing medical text and generating structured clinical insights that directly assist patients in making informed healthcare decisions.

2.1 REVIEW OF EXISTING WORKS

Study 1: Rajpurkar, P., Chen, E., Banerjee, O., and Topol, E. J. (2022). AI in health and medicine. Nature Medicine, 28(1), 31-38.
This comprehensive review examines the current state of AI applications in healthcare, covering diagnostic imaging, natural language processing of clinical notes, drug discovery, and clinical decision support. The authors note that while AI systems have shown promising results in controlled settings, significant challenges remain in clinical deployment including data privacy, model interpretability, and regulatory approval. DocNex builds upon this foundation by applying LLM-based NLP specifically to patient-facing medical report interpretation rather than clinician-facing tools.

Study 2: Lee, J., Yoon, W., Kim, S., Kim, D., Kim, S., So, C. H., and Kang, J. (2020). BioBERT: a pre-trained biomedical language representation model for biomedical text mining. Bioinformatics, 36(4), 1234-1240.
BioBERT demonstrated that pre-training language models on biomedical corpora significantly improves performance on biomedical NLP tasks including named entity recognition, relation extraction, and question answering. While BioBERT focuses on research-grade biomedical text, DocNex applies the more general-purpose LLaMA 3.3 model to patient-level medical reports, which contain a mix of medical terminology and lay language.

Study 3: Topol, E. J. (2019). High-performance medicine: the convergence of human and artificial intelligence. Nature Medicine, 25(1), 44-56.
Topol's landmark paper argues that AI will transform healthcare by augmenting doctor capabilities rather than replacing them. The paper highlights the potential of AI in pattern recognition across medical images, electronic health records, and genomic data. DocNex aligns with this vision by positioning AI as a tool that helps patients understand their reports and navigate to the right specialist, augmenting the healthcare journey rather than replacing clinical judgment.

Study 4: Sharma, A., and Kumar, R. (2021). Doctor recommendation system using machine learning. International Journal of Computer Applications, 183(12), 1-6.
This paper presents a machine learning-based doctor recommendation system that uses patient symptoms to recommend appropriate specialists. The system uses collaborative filtering and content-based filtering techniques. DocNex differs by using a keyword-based scoring algorithm that matches AI-extracted medical findings against doctor specialization tags, providing a more deterministic and explainable recommendation process.

Study 5: Agrawal, A., and Choudhary, A. (2023). NLP-based medical report analysis: A systematic review. Journal of Biomedical Informatics, 139, 104301.
This systematic review covers NLP techniques applied to medical report analysis, including information extraction, text classification, and summarization. The review identifies that most existing systems focus on specific report types (radiology, pathology) and use specialized models. DocNex takes a generalist approach using a large language model that can handle any type of medical report.

2.2 IDENTIFIED RESEARCH GAPS AND KEY FINDINGS

Gap 1 - No Free AI-Based Report Analysis for Indian Patients: Existing medical AI tools such as IBM Watson Health, Google Health, and Microsoft Azure Health Insights are enterprise-grade solutions designed for hospitals and clinics, not for direct patient use. There is no freely available tool that allows an Indian patient to upload a PDF medical report and receive an AI-powered analysis with doctor recommendations.

Gap 2 - No System Mapping Reports to AIIMS Specialists: While platforms like Practo, Apollo 24x7, and 1mg provide doctor listings, none of them analyze the patient's actual medical report to recommend the most appropriate specialist. Furthermore, no existing system specifically maps medical findings to AIIMS doctors across multiple campuses in India.

Gap 3 - Limited PDF Processing in Healthcare Apps: Most existing healthcare applications require manual data entry of symptoms and conditions. Very few applications support direct PDF upload and automated text extraction for analysis. DocNex addresses this by using client-side PDF processing with pdfjs-dist.

Gap 4 - No Integrated Analysis-to-Appointment Pipeline: Existing systems separate the processes of report analysis, doctor discovery, and appointment booking into different applications. DocNex integrates all three into a single unified workflow where a patient uploads a report, receives AI analysis, gets doctor recommendations, and can proceed to book an appointment in one seamless flow.

Gap 5 - Lack of Structured Output from Medical AI: Many medical AI chatbots provide unstructured text responses that are difficult to process programmatically. DocNex uses structured JSON prompting to ensure the AI returns precisely formatted data with 14 specific fields that can be directly rendered in the UI and used for doctor matching.

2.3 MOTIVATION FOR THE PROPOSED SYSTEM

The motivation for DocNex stems from the intersection of three factors: the identified research gaps in existing healthcare AI systems, the accessibility challenges faced by patients in the Indian public healthcare system, and the rapid maturity of large language models that now make real-time medical text analysis feasible.

India's AIIMS network treats millions of patients annually, yet many patients arrive at incorrect departments due to misunderstanding their reports or receiving incorrect referrals. A system that can read a patient's medical report and intelligently recommend the most appropriate AIIMS specialist with complete contact details and OPD schedules has the potential to significantly reduce misreferrals, optimize OPD utilization, and improve patient outcomes.

The availability of free-tier AI inference services like Groq, combined with client-side PDF processing capabilities, makes it possible to build this system without significant infrastructure costs, ensuring it can remain free for patients. This aligns with the principles of Universal Health Coverage as outlined in India's National Health Policy 2017 and the United Nations SDG Goal 3 (Good Health and Well-Being).

3.4 ALGORITHM AND PROCESS FLOW

The DocNex system employs a two-stage processing pipeline: AI-powered text analysis followed by keyword-based doctor matching.

Stage 1 - PDF Text Extraction:
Step 1: The uploaded PDF file is read as an ArrayBuffer using the Browser File API.
Step 2: pdfjs-dist loads the document and iterates through each page.
Step 3: Text content is extracted from each page using getTextContent() which returns an array of text items with their positions.
Step 4: Text items are joined with appropriate spacing to form a continuous text string.
Step 5: The complete extracted text is truncated to 1500 characters to stay within Groq's free tier token limits.

Stage 2 - AI Medical Analysis:
Step 1: The truncated medical text is embedded into a structured prompt with a system message defining the AI's role as a "senior medical AI assistant."
Step 2: The user message requests extraction of exactly 14 JSON fields including patientName, age, gender, bloodGroup, symptoms, diagnosedConditions, abnormalFindings, criticalValues, medications, severity, recommendedSpecialistType, recommendedDepartment, urgency, keyFindings, summary, and followUpRequired.
Step 3: The prompt is sent to Groq's API endpoint (https://api.groq.com/openai/v1/chat/completions) with model llama-3.3-70b-versatile, temperature 0.1, and max_tokens 400.
Step 4: The response JSON is cleaned (removing any markdown code fences) and parsed.
Step 5: Each field is validated with safe defaults (empty arrays for lists, "N/A" for strings, "moderate" for severity, "routine" for urgency).

Stage 3 - Doctor Matching Algorithm:
Step 1: A keyword pool is constructed by joining all extracted fields: recommendedSpecialistType, recommendedDepartment, diagnosedConditions array, symptoms array, and abnormalFindings array. All text is converted to lowercase.
Step 2: The keyword pool is split into individual words (minimum 3 characters) for fine-grained matching.
Step 3: For each of the 20 AIIMS doctors, a score is calculated:
  - For each specialization tag of the doctor:
    - If the tag appears directly in the keyword pool: +100 points (exact match)
    - If a pool word appears within the tag or vice versa: +40 points (partial match)
  - For the department name:
    - If any pool word appears in the department: +30 points (department match)
Step 4: Doctors with score greater than 0 are filtered and sorted in descending order of score.
Step 5: The top 3 doctors are returned as recommendations.
Step 6: If no doctors match (score = 0 for all), the first 3 doctors from the database are returned as a fallback with an isDefault flag set to true, and a notification is shown to the user.

4.3 PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS

Groq API Response Time: The Groq API with LLaMA 3.3 70B Versatile model provides inference at approximately 500+ tokens per second through their LPU (Language Processing Unit) hardware. Average response time for a 1500-character medical text analysis is 2-4 seconds, which is significantly faster than traditional GPU-based inference services.

PDF Text Extraction Speed: The pdfjs-dist library processes typical medical reports (1-10 pages) in under 1 second on modern browsers. Client-side processing eliminates network latency for the extraction step, as the PDF never leaves the user's browser.

Doctor Matching Speed: The keyword scoring algorithm processes all 20 doctors in under 1 millisecond, as it involves simple string matching operations. This ensures instant recommendations immediately after the AI response is received.

UI Rendering Performance: React 19's automatic batching and the use of useCallback/useMemo hooks for expensive computations ensure that the UI remains responsive during analysis. The Tailwind CSS utility classes are compiled at build time, resulting in a minimal CSS bundle.

Free Tier Limitations and Solutions:
- Groq Free Tier: Limited to approximately 30 requests per minute and 14,400 requests per day. The 1500-character truncation and 400 max token settings were chosen to maximize efficiency within these limits.
- Firebase Free Tier: Supports up to 10,000 authentications per month, which is sufficient for a prototype/academic project.
- Solution Applied: Text truncation from full PDF content to 1500 characters ensures the prompt fits within free tier token limits while retaining enough medical context for accurate analysis.

4.4 DISCUSSION

Challenge 1 - AI API Selection and Migration:
The project initially used Google Gemini 2.0 Flash API for medical report analysis. However, repeated 404 errors on the generateContent endpoint and quota exhaustion issues made it unreliable. The project was migrated to Gemini 3 Flash, which also encountered issues with response quality and deprecated model names. Finally, the project switched to Groq Cloud API with Meta LLaMA 3.3 70B Versatile, which provided reliable, fast, and accurate responses. This migration required updating the API endpoint, authentication mechanism, request format, and response parsing logic.

Challenge 2 - Token Limit Management:
Medical PDF reports can contain thousands of characters of text. Sending the full text to the AI model would exceed free tier token limits and increase response time. The solution was to truncate the extracted text to 1500 characters, which was found through testing to contain sufficient medical information (typically the patient demographics, primary findings, and key results) for accurate analysis. The max response tokens were set to 400 to ensure the complete JSON response fits within limits.

Challenge 3 - Structured JSON Output from LLM:
Large language models tend to add explanatory text, markdown formatting, or partial responses. The system prompt was carefully engineered with explicit instructions: "Always return ONLY valid JSON. No markdown. No explanation. No extra text." Additionally, the temperature was set to 0.1 to minimize creative variation. A regex-based cleanup step removes any accidental markdown code fences before JSON parsing. Safe defaults are applied to all 14 fields to handle cases where the model omits fields.

Challenge 4 - Doctor Matching Accuracy:
Initial keyword matching used only the recommendedSpecialistType field, which sometimes resulted in incorrect matches when the AI returned generic terms like "General Physician." The solution was to implement multi-field matching that combines data from recommendedSpecialistType, recommendedDepartment, diagnosedConditions, symptoms, and abnormalFindings into a comprehensive keyword pool. The weighted scoring system (100/40/30 points) prioritizes exact specialization matches while still considering partial keyword overlaps and department-level matches.

Challenge 5 - UI Consistency Across Pages:
Maintaining a consistent dark theme across 15+ pages required careful design system management. Tailwind CSS custom tokens for background colors (background-dark, background-light), primary color, and consistent typography (font-display using Inter) were defined globally and applied uniformly. All pages use the same card styling, button variants, and spacing patterns to ensure visual coherence.

Challenge 6 - Client-Side PDF Processing:
The pdfjs-dist library requires a web worker for PDF rendering. Configuring the worker path correctly in a Vite + React environment required setting the workerSrc property to the appropriate CDN URL. Additionally, some scanned PDF reports (image-based rather than text-based) return empty text, which is handled with a clear error message directing users to try a different file.

CONCLUSION

DocNex successfully demonstrates the practical application of Large Language Models in healthcare by creating an end-to-end pipeline from medical report upload to AI analysis to specialist doctor recommendation. The project achieves all six stated objectives:

First, the AI-powered PDF analysis system using Groq's LLaMA 3.3 70B model accurately extracts 14 structured medical fields from uploaded reports, including patient demographics, symptoms, conditions, abnormal findings, critical values, medications, severity, urgency, and specialist recommendations.

Second, the multi-field AIIMS doctor recommendation engine effectively maps AI-extracted medical findings to appropriate specialists across 7 AIIMS campuses in India, providing patients with actionable information including exact location addresses, OPD schedules, and contact numbers.

Third, the secure record management system with Firebase Google Authentication enables patients to safely upload and manage their medical documents with industry-standard OAuth 2.0 security.

Fourth, the role-based platform supports both Patient and Doctor workflows with dedicated dashboards, navigation, and feature sets.

Fifth, the premium dark-themed UI built with React 19, TypeScript, and Tailwind CSS 4 provides a modern, accessible, and responsive experience across all devices.

Sixth, the project demonstrates that LLM-based NLP can effectively process real medical reports and generate clinically relevant structured insights that directly assist patients in healthcare decision-making.

DocNex contributes to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 3 (Good Health and Well-Being) by making specialist healthcare more accessible to patients across India. By reducing the knowledge gap between complex medical reports and appropriate specialist doctors, the platform empowers patients to make informed healthcare decisions and reach the right AIIMS specialist faster.

The project also demonstrates the viability of building AI-powered healthcare tools using freely available cloud services (Groq free tier, Firebase free tier) and open-source technologies (React, pdfjs-dist), making such solutions accessible to developers and institutions with limited budgets.
